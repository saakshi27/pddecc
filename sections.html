Cantonment

There are 62 Cantonments in the Country out of which, 25 Cantonments are under the jurisdiction of Central Command. The Cantonments have been notified under Cantonments Act 1924 succeeded by Cantonments Act 2006. The overall municipal administration of the notified Cantonments is the function of the Cantonment Boards which are democratic bodies. The Station Commander of the Cantonment is the ex-officio President of the Board and an officer of the IDES or Defence Estates Organization is the Chief Executive Officer who is also the Member-Secretary of the Board. There are 4 categories of Cantonments Viz categories I to IV and number of Cantonment Board members differs according to its class.

Principal Dte DE, CC is an inter service organization between the Cantonment Boards/Army HQRs at Command Level and DG DE which deals with different Subjects relating to Cantonment Boards Viz Annual Budget estimates, Service Charges, Economy in expenditure, Special Act/Rules and Allied matters, Annual Action Plan and monitoring of Cantt Board Administration.

PDDE ensure implementation of rules/regulations and policy matters relating to Cantonment Board and Cantonment Boards are kept aware of all new instructions/policy received from the higher authorities with a view to their strict follow up

Lands

The total area measuring 240480 acres of Defence Land situated in various parts of the country falls under the jurisdiction of Central Command. An area measuring 67830 acres falls inside the Cantonment and 172650 acres are situated outside Cantonment. Necessary entries in the Govt record i.e. GLR/MLR have been updated. Necessary guidelines have been given to field Offices for survey and demarcation of Defence land. However, the responsibility of day to day management of Defence land is with the DEO / user services.

On Land matters PDDE provides advisory inputs to HQRs Central Command / LMA as and when sought for. Properties situated inside the Cantt are held on lease/old grant and are managed by CEO/DEOs. While dealing with such cases problem /querries raised by the field offices are solved and guidelines are given to proceed further strictly in accordance with the rules and regulations framed by the Govt from time to time. Any instructions / policy matter that are received from the higher formations are immediately circulated to field offices for implementation thereof.

A major problem now-a-days is of unauthorized construction and encroachment on defence land particularly on the vacant land and streets of civil area of the Cantonment. To stop such unauthorized/illegal activities, field offices i.e. DEO/CEO are repeatedly directed by PDDE to take effective steps for removal of unauthorized construction / encroachments from the defence land.

Acquisition / Hiring

Defence Estates Department is a nodal agency for acquisition of land for, Army, Air Force, Navy and DRDO. Any project where acquisition of land is involved relating to above organizations is principally approved by the Govt. and then the concerned organization convenes a Board of officers including a member of the respective DEO Circle.

After completion of Board proceedings the proposal for the requisite land acquisition is initiated by the DEO (annexed with relevant documents showing the estimated cost of land and rehabilitation charges where involved.) alongwith his comments and recommendations for obtaining govt. sanction.

At the PDDE level the proposal is examined at length and transmitted to DG DE. The land acquisition is processed under LA Act, 1894. On receipt of Govt. sanction DEO places the demand before the Collector/LAO for acquisition of land for the proposed project. Following the due process laid down in the LA Act ie. Publication of notification under section 4(1) and 6 draft award is sent by the concerned civil authorities for declaration of the final award. If the amount is required more than the sanctioned amount excess funds are placed with the DEO for remitting the same in favour of the Collector/LAO for declaration of final award.

After declaration of final award and after the required compensation is placed with the concerned LAO the possession of the acquired land is taken and handed over to the user unit simultaneously. DEO soon after taking over of possession makes necessary entries in the MLR and Raksha Bhoomi Database.

If at all any court case arises during the acquisition process or after the completion of acquisition proceedings it is defended by the LAO/DEO.

In addition to the acquisition of land there are several properties (land and building) held on hiring for which rentals are paid by the DEO Offices by making budgetary provisions in the respective budgets.

Administration

PD DE issues advisory & inputs on Admin matters to Lower formations (09 DEOs & 25 CEOs) and seeks advise/furnishes information to Higher formation i.e. DG DE. At the Command level PD DE looks after :-

  • Implementation of various policies, guidelines and directions issued by DG DE/MoD ;
  • PDDE, CC ensures implementation of orders of DG DE relating to promotions, MACP, Transfer/Posting, Training, recruitment of Group ‘C’ & ‘D’ employees and personnel matters of officers & staff posted in its jurisdiction.
  • PDDE, CC also makes transfer/posting of Group ‘C’ & ‘D’ officials within Command Jurisdiction within its delegated limits.
  • PDDE, CC keep administrative control over 25 CEOs & 9 DEOs posted in various offices of Central Command including the officers posted in Directorate, Central Command.
  • PDDE, CC is the Appointing Authority & Disciplinary Authority in respect of Group ‘C’ staff posted in Dte & 09 DEO offices.
  • PDDE, CC is the Chief Advisor of GOC-in-C & A.O-C-in-C.
  • PDDE, CC ensures allocation of funds among 9 DEOs under various budget heads & keeps control over it.
  • PDDE,CC is delegated with the power to sanction the leave to the officers posted in Central Command, issue of Move Sanction, GPF Sanction, Medical re-imbursement Sanction etc.

Rajbhasha

Hindi Cell is responsible for encouragement of the Raj Bhasha Hindi in the Directorate and all field offices. The Cell ensures strict implementation of the orders issued by the Rajbhasha, Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India in all the offices of Command Jurisdiction. The cell also carries out translation of all required texts of English & Hindi vice-versa. The Hindi Cell arranges a Hindi Pakhwara every year successfully in which different Hindi competitions viz Essay writing, noting, drafting and word meaning are organized and employees are encouraged to participate. The successful officials are motivated with the prizes provided as per guidelines issued by Govt of India, Min of Home Affairs, Rajbhasha Vibhag.
